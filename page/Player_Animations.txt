<p>Player model animations have been largely enhanced in Garry's Mod 13 (update 143). The movement system was switched to 9-way (used in Team Fortress 2), current sequences were polished/tweaked, IK rules added, and last but not least, new animations were added, which gamemodes can use for added eyecandy!
</p><p>Keep in mind that this effectively segregates NPC models and playermodels, as the former use 8-way. <b>This makes most of the Half-Life 2 NPC animations incompatible with the player animation system.</b>
</p>

<h1> <span class="mw-headline" id="For_creators"> For creators </span></h1>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Playermodels"> Playermodels </span></h2>
<p>The skeleton used is the standard HL2 ValveBiped. It can be either Valve's male or female variants.
</p><p>Playermodel creators should <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://developer.valvesoftware.com/wiki/$includemodel">include</a> the following in their QCs:
</p>
<ul><li> <code><b>m_anm.mdl</b></code> (men)
</li><li> <code><b>f_anm.mdl</b></code> (women)
</li><li> <code><b>z_anm.mdl</b></code> (zombies)
</li></ul>
<p>Make sure that your model's QC does not have any sequences that are tied to activities (including ACT_IDLE), or they may override the actual animations. As for making playermodels, the Valve Developer Wiki covers this already; nevertheless, here are <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://github.com/robotboy655/gmod-animations/raw/master/gm_anim_includes.7z">helpful .QCI includes</a>!
</p>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Animators"> Animators </span></h2>
<p>A QC source set is available here: <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://github.com/robotboy655/gmod-animations/raw/master/gm_anim_src.7z">https://github.com/robotboy655/gmod-animations/raw/master/gm_anim_src.7z</a>
</p><p>It contains examples for all movement types. You can then use this in your own player models (it is technically impossible at the moment to *add* onto the existing animations, sadly).
</p>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taunts_.26_gestures"> Taunts &amp; gestures</span></h2>
<p>Taunts are animations which were done with "no items held by the model" in mind. As such, weapons should be hidden while these activities play.
</p>
<ul><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_TAUNT_CHEER</b></code> <i>("hell yeah")</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_TAUNT_LAUGH</b></code> <i>(self explanatory)</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_TAUNT_LAUGH</b></code> <i>(also self explanatory)</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_TAUNT_MUSCLE</b></code> <i>(long "sexy" dance)</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_TAUNT_PERSISTENCE</b></code> <i>(wip, unused)</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_TAUNT_ZOMBIE</b></code> <i>(zombie tantrum)</i>
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_AGREE</b></code> <i>(thumbs up)</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_BECON</b></code> <i>(waving to say "come on")</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_DISAGREE</b></code> <i>(waving the finger to say "no")</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_SALUTE</b></code> <i>(slightly silly military-style salute)</i>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_WAVE</b></code> <i>(waving to say "hello")</i>
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_ITEM_DROP</b></code>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_ITEM_GIVE</b></code>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_ITEM_PLACE</b></code>
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_GESTURE_ITEM_THROW</b></code>
</li></ul>
<p>The above are usually accessible by any player by using the "act" command, e.g. <code>act salute</code> — unless the gamemode restricts it.
</p><p>Gmod will, by default, switch to a taunt cam while playing these.
</p>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Other"> Other </span></h2>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Multiplayer-oriented_activities"> Multiplayer-oriented activities </span></h2>
<ul><li> <code><b>ACT_LAND</b></code> links to a delta sequence that adds recoil to the player when landing from a jump.
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_IN_CHAT</b></code> will put the player's left hand to his ear. This is used when typing.
</li><li> <code><b>ACT_GMOD_DEATH</b></code> links to <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://img.maxofs2d.net/gif/gmod_death_01.gif">death animations</a> (think TF2-style). The actual code implementation is up to you.
</li></ul>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="9-way_and_8-way"> 9-way and 8-way </span></h2>
<ul><li> 9-way: two pose parameters, move_x and move_y, control the direction and "intensity" of the movement.
</li><li> 8-way: a single pose parameter, move_yaw, controls the angle of the movement.
</li></ul>
<p>The main difference is that 9-way allows for smooth transitions between idle and its closest movement states. The player animations were switched to 9-way for Gmod 13; Half-Life 2's NPCs, however, are still 8-way.
</p>
