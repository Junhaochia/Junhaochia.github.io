<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_remove_props"> How to remove props </span></h2>
<p><a href="/page/File:removing_props.png" class="image"><img alt="removing props.png" src="/images/2/2c/removing_props.png" width="625" height="464" /></a>
</p>
<ol><li> Hold <span class="keyboard-key" style="display: inline-block; background-color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.1);border-radius: 4px;text-align: center; color: #000; padding: 0.2em 0.5em; font-size: 0.85em;">C</span>,
</li><li> hover over the prop you want to remove,
</li><li> <span class="keyboard-key" style="display: inline-block; background-color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.1);border-radius: 4px;text-align: center; color: #000; padding: 0.2em 0.5em; font-size: 0.85em;"><span title="Right mouse button"><img alt="RMB" src="/images/0/02/Rmb.png" width="16" height="16" /></span></span> Right click on the prop,
</li><li> Select "Remove"
</li><li> Done!
</li></ol>
<p>Alternatively you might want to use the <a href="/page/Tools/Remover" title="Tools/Remover">remover tool</a>.
</p><p><br />

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Spawning_other_type_of_props"> Spawning other type of props </span></h2>
<p>Aside from regular props you can spawn other stuff.
A list of items:
</p>
<ul><li> <b>Weapons</b> - This is quite obvious
</li><li> <b>Entities</b> - These are scripted entities or if you want: props that perform a task. By default Garry's Mod comes with a bouncy ball and a bunch of HL2 entities
</li><li> <b>NPCs</b> - Computer controlled allies, enemies and neutrals. Including but not limited to Citizens, Birds, Combine Soldiers and Striders
</li><li> <b>Vehicles</b> - Vehicles that you can drive or sit in.
</li></ul>
<p>The spawn menu has 2 other tabs, <b>Post Process</b> and <b>Creations</b>.
</p>
<ul><li> <b>Post Process</b> consists of a list of effects, such as Depth of Field (blur), that can be used to either enhance gameplay or make screenshots better. Note that these require extra calculations to be done and might slow down your game.
</li><li> <b>Creations</b> contains a list of Workshop items which people have created. There are 2 categories, Saves and Dupes
<ul><li> Saves are, as you may have guessed, map saves. These record all locations of all entities and load them when you click on "Load".
</li><li> Dupes are creations people have made and have put on the Workshop. When you load these the duplicator tool will open and will spawn whatever you loaded from the Workshop when you click the mouse.
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
