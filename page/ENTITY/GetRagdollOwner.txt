<h1> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h1>
<p>Returns the entity which the ragdoll came from. The opposite of <a href="/page/Category_colon_Player" title="Category:Player">Player</a>:<a href="/page/Player/GetRagdollEntity" title="Player/GetRagdollEntity">GetRagdollEntity</a>.
</p>
<h1 class="first_ret"> <span class="mw-headline" id="Returns">Returns</span></h1>
<div class="return" style="counter-increment: returns;">
<p><span class="ret_number" style="counter-increment: returns -1;"></span> <span class="ret_chunk"><a href="/page/Category_colon_Entity" title="Category:Entity">Entity</a></span>
</p>
<div style="margin-left: 32px;">
The entity who owns the ragdoll.</div>
</div>
<h1 class="first_example"> <span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span></h1>
<h2 class="examples_number"> <span class="mw-headline" id="Example">Example</span></h2>
<p>Loop through all player ragdolls and print their owners.
</p>
<pre><code data-language="lua">for e, ent in pairs( ents.GetAll() ) do
	if( IsValid( ent:GetRagdollOwner() ) ) then
		print( ent:GetRagdollOwner() )
	end
end</code></pre>
<p><b>Output:</b>
</p>
<div style="margin:2em;">While a player is dead and their ragdoll is spawned this returns: <br />Player [1][PlayerName]</div>
