<tr>
<td> <a href="/page/Category_colon_table" title="Category:table">table</a>
</td>
<td> offcenter
</td>
<td>
<p>This allows you to "zoom in" on a part of the screen - for example, the top-left quarter of the screen. This is similar to how <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://garry.tv/2012/02/25/poster-screenshots/">poster splits the screen</a> into separate renders.
</p><p>It's a table with 4 keys, controlling what portion of the screen to draw:
</p>
<ul><li> left - where the left edge starts. Natural value is 0.
</li><li> right - where the right edge ends. Natural value is equal to w (the width of the viewport).
</li><li> top - where the <i>bottom</i> edge starts. Natural value is 0.
</li><li> bottom - where the <i>top</i> edge ends. Natural value is equal to h (the height of the viewport).
</li></ul>
<p>Note that top and bottom are reversed.
</p><p>Values outside the viewport are allowed, but not recommended - instead you should increase the view FOV.
</p>
</td></tr></table>
