<p><b>Scripted Entities</b> (or <b>SENT</b>s for short) are custom entities coded entirely in Lua.
</p><p>The difference from <b>Engine Entities</b> being that you cannot use <a href="/page/Category_colon_ENTITY_Hooks" title="Category:ENTITY Hooks">SENT Hooks</a> on engine entities.
</p>
<h1> <span class="mw-headline" id="Scripted_Entity_Types">Scripted Entity Types</span></h1>
<p>There are 6 different types of Scripted Entities:
</p>
<table class="wikitable">
<tr>
<td>ai
</td>
<td>A scripted NPC. (Also known as SNPCs)<br />All <a href="/page/Category_colon_NPC" title="Category:NPC">NPC</a> functions and certain AI hooks are also usable on these entities.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>nextbot
</td>
<td>A NextBot NPC. A newer "style" NPCs with better navigation.<br />All <a href="/page/Category_colon_NextBot" title="Category:NextBot">NextBot</a> functions and <a href="/page/Category_colon_NEXTBOT_Hooks" title="Category:NEXTBOT Hooks">NEXTBOT</a> hooks are also usable on these entities.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>anim
</td>
<td>A normal entity with visual and/or physical presence in the game world, such as props or whatever else you can imagine.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>brush
</td>
<td>A serverside only trigger entity. Mostly used very closely with the Hammer Level Editor.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>point
</td>
<td>A usually serverside only entity that doesn't have a visual or physical representation in the game world, such as logic entities.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>filter
</td>
<td>A different kind of "point" entity used in conjunction with trigger ("brush" type) entities.
</td></tr></table>
